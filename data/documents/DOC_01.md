Title: Digital Platforms and the Variance of Stress-Anxiety Outcomes
Theme: Evaluating the correlation between specific social media platform usage and psychological distress levels.

Summary:
Analysis of the dataset reveals that the choice of social media platform correlates with distinct mental health profiles among adolescents. While TikTok usage is associated with the highest average anxiety scores, users engaging with multiple platforms ("Both") report the highest levels of physiological stress. These findings suggest that the nature of platform interaction, rather than just total duration, plays a role in the manifestation of specific mental health symptoms.

Key Findings:
- TikTok-exclusive users demonstrate the highest average anxiety levels at 5.75/10, outperforming both Instagram-only and multi-platform users.
- Adolescents utilizing "Both" Instagram and TikTok concurrently report the highest average stress levels (5.55/10), indicating a possible "platform switching" cognitive load.
- Instagram-only users maintain a mid-range anxiety profile (5.67/10) but show stress levels (5.50/10) significantly higher than TikTok-only users (5.29/10).
- The dataset indicates that while TikTok may drive higher anxiety, it correlates with the lowest mean stress levels among the three usage categories.
- High-frequency social media users (6+ hours) are distributed across all platforms, but those on TikTok show a more pronounced skew toward upper-decile anxiety scores.

Evidence Snapshot:

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

Interpretation:
The divergence between TikTok-induced anxiety and multi-platform stress suggests that different algorithmic or social structures impact teens uniquely. The high anxiety on TikTok may be linked to the rapid-fire nature of the content feed, while the higher stress levels in users of "Both" platforms might stem from the pressure to maintain social presence across multiple digital environments.

Limitations:
- The data does not account for the specific content consumed.
- Self-reported stress and anxiety levels may be subject to subjective bias.
- Platform usage categories are broad.
