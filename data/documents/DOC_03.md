Document ID: DOC_03
Title: Pre-Sleep Screen Exposure and Behavioral Addiction Links
Theme: Analyzing the impact of screen time immediately before sleep on addiction and mood.

Summary:
High levels of screen time before sleep are positively correlated with behavioral addiction and a higher incidence of depression markers. The final three hours of the day are a high-risk window where digital engagement disrupts stability. Teens with more than 2.5 hours of pre-sleep screen exposure show the highest susceptibility to social media addiction.

Key Findings:
- Teens with 2-3 hours of pre-sleep screen time report an average addiction level of 5.78, compared to 5.40 for those with less than 1 hour.
- The incidence of the depression label is approximately 13% higher in the 2-3 hour screen time group than in the 1-2 hour group.
- Academic performance shows a slight downward trend as pre-sleep screen time increases.
- Over 60% of teens with high addiction levels (8-10) report at least 2.0 hours of screen time before bed.
- There is a direct linear relationship between screen time before sleep and total daily social media hours.

Evidence Snapshot:
| Screen Time Before Sleep (Hrs) | Avg Addiction Level | Avg Depression Label | Avg Academic Performance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 0.5 - 1.0 | 5.40 | 0.026 | 3.05 |
| 1.5 - 2.0 | 5.46 | 0.024 | 2.98 |
| 2.5 - 3.0 | 5.78 | 0.027 | 2.92 |

Interpretation:
The findings indicate that digital engagement immediately before sleep acts as a catalyst for addiction. This suggests that the psychological "cooling down" period is vital; when replaced by screen time, the ability to regulate mood is compromised.

Limitations:
- Does not specify platforms used during the pre-sleep window.
- Interaction with blue light sensitivity is not measured.
- Overlap with total daily screen time makes isolating the timing effect difficult.
