Title: Comparative Profile Analysis: High-Risk vs. Low-Risk Adolescents
Theme: A multi-factor comparison of behavior patterns in extreme mental health outcomes.

Summary:
Isolating the polar extremes identifies a "High-Risk" profile characterized by severe sleep deprivation and excessive social media use. High-risk teens spend nearly 50% more time on social media and sleep 1.8 hours less than low-risk counterparts. This highlights the compounding effects of behavioral choices.

Key Findings:
- High-risk teens average 6.72 hours of social media daily; low-risk teens average 4.55 hours.
- High-risk individuals sleep only 4.76 hours on average; low-risk individuals sleep 6.59 hours.
- High-risk profiles show addiction levels frequently hitting 9 or 10.
- Low-risk teens engage in slightly more physical activity (1.01 hours vs 0.95 hours).
- The GPA of the low-risk group is significantly higher (~3.2 vs ~2.6).

Evidence Snapshot:
| Metric | High-Risk Mean (N=31) | Low-Risk Mean (N=109) | Dataset Mean |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Social Media Hours | 6.72 | 4.55 | 4.54 |
| Sleep Hours | 4.76 | 6.59 | 6.45 |
| Stress Level | 8.42 | 1.95 | 5.45 |

Interpretation:
Mental health issues arise from a "perfect storm" of high digital consumption and extreme sleep deprivation. Interventions targeting a reduction in social media hours and an increase in sleep are likely to yield the best outcomes.

Limitations:
- Small sample size for the High-Risk group (N=31).
- Risk definitions are for analytical purposes and not clinical diagnosis.
- Analysis shows correlation, not causation.
